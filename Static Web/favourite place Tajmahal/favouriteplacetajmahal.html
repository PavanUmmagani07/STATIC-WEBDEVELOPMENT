Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Agra, India, is a timeless masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Built in the 17th century by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, it stands as a symbol of eternal love. Its stunning white marble facade, intricate minarets, and exquisite inlay work make it one of the most iconic landmarks globally and a testament to the skill and artistry of the artisans who created it. The Taj Mahal's beauty is further enhanced by the serene reflection pools and lush gardens surrounding it, making it a must-visit destination for travelers from all over the world.
